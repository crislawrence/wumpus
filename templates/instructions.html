Instructions

Scenario

You are a hunter seeking to kill a dangerous beast know as a wumpus. The wumpus lives in a warren composed of 20 interconnected caves. Each cave adjoins three other caves. You have a quiver connecting 5 arrows which you will use to kill the wumpus.

There are additional dangerous hazards in the cavern system: bottomless pits and bat colonies. If you enter a cave with a bottomless pit, you will die. If you enter a cave housing a bat colony, the bats will collectively pick you up and drop you in some other cave. The danger is the cave in which you are deposited may either have a bottomless pit or the wumpus. The wumpus is asleep somewhere in the cavern system at the start of the game. If you enter the cave with the wumpus, the wumpus will awaken and promptly eat you!

In addition to the cave you are in, you something about the three adjoining caves. If any of those neighboring cave contain a bottomless pit, you will feel a breeze. If any contain a bat colony, you will hear the flapping of wings. The wumpus, on the other hand, can be detected when it is within two caves of you because its awful odor extends that far.

Be aware that the wumpus may be residing in a cave with a bat colony because the wumpus is too heavy for the bats to life. The wumpus may also be residing in a cave with a bottomless pit because the wumpus has sucker feet and can stick to the sides of the bottomless pit. In that case, you won't fall into the abyss if you enter. The wumpus will awaken, pluck you out of the air and eat you!

To kill the wumpus, you must shoot an arrow into the cave in which it is residing. That means that the wumpus must be in one of the caves adjoining the one you are in. If you miss the wumpus, the wumpus will awaken and start to move about. Once awake, every time you shoot an arrow or enter a cave, the wumpus will also enter another cave. The wumpus can only enter a adjoining cave for each move as it cannot fly.

Field Notes

The field notes display the parts of the cavern system that you have mapped, including where you encountered the feeling, sound, and/or smell associated with each of the various dangers. But once the wumpus is awake and moving, past indications on the map of the wumpus' location are removed since those notes are no longer relevant.

The caves for which nearby hazard have been identified are in yellow. Those caves known but as yet unexplored are in black. Those caves having no nearby hazards are in green. The cave the hunter is currently within is identified with an asterisk.
