Description and Rules

Objective

You are a hunter seeking a dangerous beast known as a Wumpus. The Wumpus lives somewhere within a underground warren composed of interconnected caves. You must track down and slay the beast while avoiding the cavern system's many hazards.

Game Board

Cavern System
  • The cavern system is composed of 20 interconnected caves labeled 1 through 20.
  • Each cave in the cavern system is linked by tunnels to exactly three other caves.
Hunter
  • This is you. The hunter starts the game is a cave containing no hazards. However, hazards may exist in neighboring caves.
  • The hunter (you) is equipped with a quiver containing 5 arrows. If you exhaust your complement of arrows without killing the Wumpus, you will be at the mercy of the Wumpus. It is just a matter of time until the Wumpus catches up to you
  • The hunter may either enter an adjoining cave or shoot an arrow into an adjoining cave.
Status and Field Notes
  • The status pane provides current information as to the yours whereabouts, whether you have encountered any hazards or whether any are close by and whether you have slain the Wumpus.
  • The field notes display the parts of the cavern system that you have mapped, including where you encountered the feeling, sound, and/or smell associated with each of the various potential dangers. Cave where nearby hazards were detected are shown in yellow. Hover over one to see what hazards were noted there.
  • Once the Wumpus is awake and moving, past indications on the map of the wumpus' location are removed since those notes are no longer relevant.
  • Those caves that are known but as yet unexplored are in black. Those caves having no nearby hazards are in green. The cave the hunter is currently within is identified with an asterisk.

Hazards

Bottomless Pit
  • There is a bottomless pit in each of two of the caves in the cavern system.
  • If you enter a cave housing a bottomless pit, you will die.
  • You can tell when a bottomless pit exists in one of the caves adjoining yours because you will feel a draft coming from it.
  • The Wumpus may also be residing in a cave with a bottomless pit because the Wumpus has sucker feet and can stick to the sides of the bottomless pit. If you should wander into such a cave, you won't fall into the abyss. Instead, the Wumpus will awaken, pluck you out of the air and eat you!
Bat Colony
  • There is a bat colony in each of two of the caves in the cavern system.
  • If you enter a cave harboring a bat colony, the bats will swoop down, pick you up, and ferry you to another cave of their choice in the cavern system. You may even be deposited in a cave containing other hazards.
  • You can tell when a bat colony exists in one of the caves adjoining yours because you will hear the flapping of wings coming from it
  • The Wumpus may also be residing in a cave with a bat colony because the Wumpus is just too heavy for the bats to lift. If you should wander into such a cave, you won't be carried off by the bats. The Wumpus will snag you for a meal instead.
Wumpus
  • Fortunately for you, there is only one Wumpus living in the cavern system. Initially the Wumpus is sleeping peacefully in its cave.
  • If you enter the cave containing the Wumpus, the Wumpus awakens and snacks on you.
  • You can tell when a Wumpus is nearby because it smells awful. In fact, its odor is so pervasive that you can detect it when the Wumpus is as much as two caves removed from your location.
  • If you shoot an arrow into the cave containing the Wumpus, you will slay the Wumpus. However, if you shoot an arrow into a cave that does not contain the Wumpus, the Wumpus will awaken and start looking for you. Fortunately for you, the Wumpus can't smell you.
  • Once you have awakened the Wumpus, it will move to another cave with every move you make. So any past evidence of its presence becomes obsolete information rather quickly.
